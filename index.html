Methodology & Data Sources

General Methodology

This project creates two main composite indices: the Healthcare Need Index and the Healthcare Access Score, both expressed on a scale of 0 to 100.

Healthcare Access Score Method (Z-Score Based)

Access metrics are standardized using Z-Score Normalization across the 36 counties: z = (x - Average) / Standard Deviation. Standardized Z-scores are aggregated into three categories and combined hierarchically:

  • Structural Capacity (45% weight): Evaluates capacity by taking the average Z-score of:
    - Hospital beds per 10,000 residents (ArcGIS Oregon Health Authority hospitals spatial join / Census population)
    - Dentists per 1,000 residents (aggregated from OHSU Unmet Need Indicators excel data, weighted by service area population)
    - Mental Health Providers per 1,000 residents (aggregated from OHSU Unmet Need Indicators excel data, weighted by service area population)
    - Primary Care Capacity Ratio (aggregated from OHSU Unmet Need Indicators excel data, weighted by service area population)
  • Geographical Accessibility (35% weight): Standardized Z-score of the travel time to the nearest Patient-Centered Primary Care Home. This is inverted (Z-score multiplied by -1) because longer travel times mean worse accessibility.
  • Service Level (20% weight): Standardized Z-score of the Highest Trauma Level Available in the county. Trauma levels are scaled as: Level 1 = 100, Level 2 = 75, Level 3 = 50, Level 4 = 25, None = 0.

The final combined Z-score is scaled using a linear Min-Max transformation to a 0–100 range for display and mapping purposes, ensuring relative relationships and variances are preserved.

Healthcare Need Score Method (Percentile Ranks)

Community need indicators are standardized using Percentile Ranks (0 to 100) to represent relative vulnerability. Indicators are organized into 5 thematic sections ordered by weight:

  • Health Outcomes Burden (35% weight): 21 variables representing chronic illnesses and quality-of-life impairments, including Diabetes, Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Disease, Heart disease, Stroke, Hypertension, Cancer, Asthma, Obesity, disability (all types), poor/fair self-rated health, cognitive/mental distress, and mobility/self-care limitations.
  • Socioeconomic Vulnerability (25% weight): 8 variables. Poverty %, Households without vehicle %, Broadband lack (inverted access rate), Food insecurity (PLACES), Housing insecurity (PLACES), Utility shutoff threats (PLACES), Uninsured rate (derived from Small Area Health Insurance Estimates in insured.csv), and Transportation barriers.
  • Preventable Care Gaps (25% weight): 9 variables. Averages the inverted percentile ranks of positive preventive screeners (Colorectal, Mammography, Cholesterol, Routine checkups, Dental visits) with the ascending percentile ranks of negative care outcomes (preventable hospitalizations, Emergency Department dental visits, Emergency Department mental health visits, inadequate prenatal care).
  • Demographics (7.5% weight): Population over 65 % and People of Color (POC) % percentile ranks.
  • Lifestyle & Behavioral Risks (7.5% weight): Smoking %, Physical inactivity %, Short sleep duration %, Binge drinking %, Loneliness %, and Lack of social/emotional support %.

For each section, sub-indicator percentile ranks are averaged. The composite Need Score is the weighted average of these five sections. The final NeedIndex (0 to 100) is the percentile rank of this weighted average across all 36 counties.
